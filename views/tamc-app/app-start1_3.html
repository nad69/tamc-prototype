Apply for Marriage Allowance

For the tax year 2015 to 2016, most people will be able to earn up to £10,600 a year before paying tax on their income which includes pensions, savings and investments. This is called a Personal Allowance.

If your income is less than £10,600 (£880 a month), Marriage Allowance lets you transfer £1,060 of your Personal Allowance to your spouse or civil partner.

This may reduce the amount of tax they pay each week or month and could be worth up to £212 a year.

Before you start

You’ll be able to claim Marriage Allowance if all the eligibility criteria apply:

  • you’re married or in a legally registered civil partnership
  • you have an annual income of less than £10,600 - including pensions,savings and investments
  • your spouse or civil partner has an annual income of between £10,601 and £42,385
  • you were both born on or after 6 April 1935

Steps to apply

1. You can use the Marriage Allowance calculator (optional) to see how much you may reduce your husband, wife or civil partner's tax by.

2. You'll need to use UK.GOV Verify. This is the government’s way of checking your identity. If you have never done this, you'll need to create an account first.

3. You'll need to give us your husband, wife or civil partner’s:

  • first name and last name
  • gender
  • National Insurance (NI) number

4. Confirm your husband, wife or civil partner’s details and apply.

It takes about 10 minutes to apply. You’ll get an email confirming your claim.
