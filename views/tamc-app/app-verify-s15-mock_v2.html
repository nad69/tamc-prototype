1. Verify to use all online government services

To transfer your Personal Allowance to your spouse or civil partner online, you'll need to use GOV.UK Verify - the new way for you to prove who you are online, so you can use government services safely.

To do this, you'll need to choose a certified company who will ask questions that only you know the answer to.

You only have to verify your identity once. After that you can sign in with your username and password and use these to sign into other online government services.

Before you continue, remember it's the person on the lower income making the transfer who should be verifying their identity.

Verify now
If you can't use GOV.UK Verify you can still apply for Marriage Allowance online by verifying your identity with HMRC (option 2) or by telephone. You'll be given the telephone option if you can't verify your identity online.

2. Verify with HMRC to apply for Marriage Allowance only

If you'd prefer to simply answer some security questions directly with HMRC, you can do this instead.

Before you continue, remember it's the person on the lower income making the transfer who should be verifying their identity.
