Apply for Marriage Allowance

For the tax year 2015 to 2016, most people will be able to earn up to £10,600 a year before paying tax on their income which includes pensions, savings and investments. This is called a Personal Allowance.

If your income is £10,600 or less (£880 a month), Marriage Allowance lets you transfer £1,060 of your Personal Allowance to your spouse or civil partner.

This may reduce the amount of tax they pay each week or month and could be worth up to £212 a year.

Before you start

You'll be able to transfer some of your Personal Allowance to your partner if all the eligibility criteria apply:

  • you’re married or in a legally registered civil partnership
  • you have an annual income of £10,600 or less - including pensions, savings and investments
  • your spouse or civil partner has an annual income of between £10,601 and £42,385
  • you were both born on or after 6 April 1935

Steps to apply for Marriage Allowance

1. You can use the Marriage Allowance calculator (optional) to see how much you may benefit as a couple.

2. You'll need access to your own information to confirm your identity with the GOV.UK Verify service or sign in if you've done this already.

3. Give us your spouse of civil partner's details to make the transfer to them (you'll need their first name, last name and National Insurance number).

4. Confirm your spouse or civil partner's details and apply.

It may take up to 20 minutes to apply, depending on whether you need to verify your identity for the first time. You'll get an email confirmation.
