Marriage Allowance

Most people can earn up to £10,600 a year before paying tax on their income which includes pensions, savings and investments. This is called a Personal Allowance.

For the tax year 2015 to 2016, if your income is less than £10,600 (£880 a month), Marriage Allowance lets you transfer £1060 of your Personal Allowance to your spouse or civil partner. This may reduce the amount of tax they pay by up to £212 annually.

Couples may benefit annually by up to£212

Register your interest

You'll be able to apply later in the year, but you can register your interest now.

Before you start

Find out if you're eligible before you register your interest.

You could transfer £1060 of your Personal Allowance to your spouse or civil partner if:

  • you're married or in a legally registered civil partnership and
  • you have an annual income of less than £10,600 a year (around £880 a month) and
  • your spouse or civil partner has an annual income of more than £10,600 but less than £42,385 a year and
  • you were both born on or after 6 April 1935

You don't have to register, but if you do we'll tell you when you can apply. Registering will only take around 3 minutes.
